## Part-A 
## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud (with Real Incident)

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing payments. Instead of hacking systems, criminals manipulate victims into willingly completing transactions.

A typical fraud begins when the victim receives a call, SMS, or WhatsApp message posing as a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. The steps usually include:

(1) building trust through impersonation

(2) sending a payment request or QR code

(3) convincing the victim that entering their UPI PIN is required to “receive” money

(4) once the PIN is entered money is instantly debited.

A real-world example occurred in Delhi, where fraudsters used stolen mobile phones to access victims’ banking apps and conduct unauthorized UPI transactions. In one case, a victim lost around ₹20 lakh after his phone was stolen and criminals gained access to his financial apps.

Students, elderly individuals, and new digital users are common targets due to limited awareness. The consequences include immediate financial loss, emotional distress, and difficulty recovering funds because UPI transactions are instant and often irreversible.

Such frauds are increasing significantly, with over 1.6 million UPI fraud cases reported in India in a single year.

Prevention includes never sharing UPI PINs or OTPs, avoiding unknown payment requests, and verifying sources before making transactions.
